## What Sets High-Efficiency Electric Water Heaters Apart?

### How Does a High-Efficiency Electric Water Heater Work?

High-efficiency electric water heaters utilize advanced insulation and heat pump technology to minimize energy consumption. Conventional models often rely purely on electrical resistance heating, which can be inefficient. In contrast, high-efficiency models capture ambient heat and enhance heating capabilities while reducing electricity use.

In essence, these systems heat water more sustainably, leading to lower energy bills—dozens of dollars each month. According to the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), switching to a high-efficiency water heater can reduce your water heating costs by as much as 25%.

### What’s the Difference Between Tank and Tankless Models?

If you're contemplating which water heater type to install, it's essential to understand the distinction between tank and tankless models:

- **Tank Models:** Generally cheaper upfront and store hot water ready for use. However, they consume energy continuously to keep the water warm.
- **Tankless Models:** These activate only when you need hot water, offering lower ongoing costs but often higher initial investment.

My preference ended up being a tankless model after factoring in long-term savings, even though the initial cost was about $1,000 more.
