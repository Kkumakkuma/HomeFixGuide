## Why Choose Electric Lawn Mowers?

Electric lawn mowers have soared in popularity, especially for homeowners with small yards. They are quieter, more eco-friendly, and lower maintenance compared to traditional gas mowers. In my experience, the convenience of simply plugging in my mower or using a battery has made yard work a lot less of a chore.

### Benefits of Electric Lawn Mowers

- **Environmental Impact**: No emissions and reduced noise pollution.
- **Lower Operating Costs**: Tend to be cheaper in the long run, saving around $200-$500 annually compared to gas mowers.
- **Ease of Use**: Lightweight and often easier to maneuver.

## Features to Consider

Before you purchase an electric mower for your small yard, consider the following features:

### Power Source: Corded vs. Cordless

- **Corded**: These mowers provide unlimited run time but can be cumbersome with cords lying across your yard. Prices typically range from $150 to $300.
- **Cordless**: While they give you more freedom, you must keep an eye on battery life. Expect to pay between $250 and $500 for quality cordless models.

### Cutting Width

For small yards, a cutting width of 14 to 20 inches is generally ideal. This provides sufficient coverage without being unwieldy.

### Weight and Portability

If your yard has several tight corners, a lightweight model (under 40 lbs) will be much easier to handle.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

Many blogs will rush to recommend electric mowers without addressing crucial considerations. Here are three insights I think are often overlooked:

1. **Battery Maintenance**: Not all batteries are created equal. Many guides fail to mention the need for proper battery care to maximize lifespan, including not letting them drain completely often.
  
2. **Size Doesn't Always Matter**: Cut width isn’t everything—efficiency in your specific yard shape is vital. The maneuverability of a mower can be just as important as its cutting width.
  
3. **Noise Levels**: While touted as quieter than gas mowers, some electric models are still noisy. Always check dB ratings if noise pollution is a concern for your neighborhood.
