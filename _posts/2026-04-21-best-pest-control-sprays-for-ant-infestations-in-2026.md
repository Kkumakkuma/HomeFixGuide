## Types of Ants and Their Weaknesses

Understanding the type of ants invading your home is crucial for effective pest control. Not all sprays work on all ant species. Here are common ant types and what I learned through my personal experience:

### Carpenter Ants

- **Habitat**: Found in decaying wood. 
- **Weakness**: They are attracted to baits with protein and sugar.
  
### Sugar Ants

- **Habitat**: Often seen in kitchens, attracted to sweets and proteins.
- **Weakness**: They respond well to liquid baits and sprays that contain sugar.

### Argentine Ants

- **Habitat**: Known for forming supercolonies, often seen in large numbers.
- **Weakness**: These are best tackled with gel baits that can reach their deep nests.

Identifying the species can help you select the best pest control sprays tailored to your infestation.

## Methods of Application: Learn from My Experience

### Direct Spraying

Direct spraying is often the quickest way to see results. When I applied Raid directly to visible ants, the immediate impact was noticeable. 

### Bait Stations

Bait stations are designed to lure ants into the product, allowing them to carry it back to their colonies. I once placed Ortho baits near the entrance and noticed a two-week reduction in their numbers. 

### Granular Treatment

Granules can be scattered around the area where ants appear. They dissolve slowly, making them a longer-lasting solution. I experienced effective results with Ortho’s granular formula that seemed to keep ants away longer than liquid sprays.
