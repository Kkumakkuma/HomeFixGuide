## Understanding Your Options: The Basics of Toilet Selection 

Choosing the best toilet isn't just about picking a pretty model; it's about functionality, water efficiency, and, of course, price. Here are a few essential factors to keep in mind when searching for a budget-friendly option for your small bathroom.

### Types of Toilets to Consider

1. **Two-Piece Toilets**: These are the most common types. The tank and bowl are separate, which can sometimes make installation easier. They typically range from $100 to $300.
  
2. **One-Piece Toilets**: Combining tank and bowl into one unit, these toilets are sleek and easier to clean but may cost between $250 and $500.

3. **Compact Toilets**: Perfect for small bathrooms, these measure about 27 to 28 inches in height and can fit in tight spaces. Prices start around $150.

### Water Efficiency Matters

Toilets are one of the largest consumers of water in our homes. Choosing a model with a low flush rate (1.28 gallons per flush or lower) not only saves money but can also help conserve water. I learned this the hard way when my old toilet, using 3.5 gallons per flush, barely dented my water bill. 

### Height and Comfort

Toilets come in different heights: standard (around 16 inches) and comfort height (you’ll see these at about 17 to 19 inches). The latter can make sitting and standing easier for those with mobility issues. When I installed the Kohler K-75809-0, a comfort height model, I truly appreciated how much easier it made life for my family.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

When I dug into various guides for remodeling bathrooms and toilet selection, I stumbled upon several common misconceptions:

### 1. Overestimating Installation Costs

Many guides emphasize that professional installation is essential, but if you're a handy DIYer, you can save as much as $200 to $300 by installing the toilet yourself. In my case, I watched YouTube tutorials and got my toilet installed in a couple of hours.

### 2. Ignoring Water Savings

The emphasis on initial costs often overshadows the long-term savings with water-efficient models. For example, switching from a 3.5 GPF toilet to a 1.28 GPF saved me approximately $80 annually in the first year, depending on local water rates.

### 3. Assuming All Toilets Fit Small Spaces

Many guides fail to mention the importance of compact toilet models. These options exist and can often accommodate unique small spaces better than standard models.

## Frequently Asked Questions 

### ### What is a good price for a budget-friendly toilet?

A budget-friendly toilet generally falls between $200 and $500, depending on the features and design. Higher-end compact toilets can go above $900, but you can often find solid models for under $300.

### ### Are low-flow toilets worth it?

Absolutely! Low-flow toilets can save you substantial amounts of money over time. I calculated that switching to a 1.28 GPF toilet saved me upwards of 2,500 gallons of water annually, which means savings on your water bill.

### ### How long do toilets typically last?

In my experience, most toilets last at least 15 to 20 years with proper maintenance. By investing in a reliable model, you’re ensuring fewer headaches and replacements down the line.

### ### How do I maintain my new toilet?

Maintaining your toilet doesn’t have to be complicated. Regular cleaning, checking for leaks, and replacing worn-out flappers can extend its lifespan significantly.
