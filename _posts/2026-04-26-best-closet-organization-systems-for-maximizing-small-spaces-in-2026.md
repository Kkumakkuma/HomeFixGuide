## Understanding Your Closet Space

### Think Vertical

When working with a small closet, it’s essential to think vertically. Every inch counts, and going upwards can significantly optimize your storage.

- **Overhead Shelving**: This is a simple solution that can offer additional space for less frequently used items. 
- **Hanging Organizers**: Utilize vertical space by adding hanging organizers for shoes, belts, or accessories. I found a fabric shoe organizer that hangs on the door—perfect for my collection of shoes that never seemed to fit on shelves.

### Evaluate Your Needs

Understanding your storage needs will guide your choice. Here are some questions to ask yourself:

1. What items do you need to store? 
2. How often do you use certain items? 
3. Do you need space for shoes, garments, or seasonal wear?

I realized my heavy winter coats and shoes took up a substantial amount of space, so I needed a system that included room for those larger items.

## Seasonal Rotation Strategies

### Mindful Seasonal Swaps

A vital component of closet organization, especially in smaller spaces, is smart seasonal rotation. I learned this the hard way. Initially, I left all my clothes hanging year-round. After a rough winter where I needed space for summer dresses, I implemented a strategy where I swap out clothes seasonally.

#### Process

1. **Assess**: At the end of each season, I review which items I used the most and which ones I can store.
2. **Store**: Invest in quality storage bins like **Sterilite 18 Gallon Storage Totes** (around $11 each) to keep out-of-season clothing clean and safe.
3. **Label**: I learned the importance of labeling bins; it cuts down the time spent searching. A simple pack of labeling tape does wonders.

This switch has made my closet feel organized year-round and significantly decreased my morning routine chaos.

### Seasonal Checklist

To make rotation easier, I’ve developed a seasonal checklist that includes:

- Categories for clothing: summer dresses, winter coats, etc.
- A disposal list for items that are worn out or no longer fit.
- A “wish list” of items I find myself missing throughout the season.

Once I put together this checklist, maintaining my closet feels less like a chore and more like a planned event that I look forward to.

### DIY Vertical Dividers
