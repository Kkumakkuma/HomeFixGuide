## The Impressive Attributes of Behr Premium Paint

### Coverage and Application

One of the standout features of Behr Premium Paint is its coverage efficiency. On average, I found that one gallon covers about 400 square feet, which is impressive compared to many competitor products. When I painted an accent wall in my living room, I only needed one coat, saving me both paint and time.

- **Opacity:** Behr Premium Paint consistently provided full opacity in a single coat, especially in deep and vibrant colors.
- **Ease of Application:** Users can appreciate how smoothly it rolls on and how quickly it dries, averaging about one hour between coats.

### Durability That Lasts

After six months, the durability of my Behr Premium paint job still impresses me. I put it to the test with various household activities, from moving furniture to cleaning spills. Compared to 2023 Valspar Paint, which started showing wear within three months, the Behr kept its fresh appearance for much longer.

**How it compares:**
- **Scrub Tests:** I found that Behr Premium withstands scrubbing better than Valspar, which can show marks after a few cleanings.
- **Stain Resistance:** Behr's resistance to stains from common items like coffee and pets is exceptional. I tested a few food stains and was happy to see that most wiped off without a trace.

### High-Quality Finish Options

Behr Premium Paint offers various finishes, from flat to high-gloss. I initially went with the eggshell finish for my living room, which gives a rich, soft look. 

**My finish choice:**

- The gloss levels adjust depending on the room’s purpose; for example, satin finishes are recommended for kitchens and bathrooms.

## Personal Experiences with Behr Premium Color Selection

### Finding the Perfect Hue

Choosing a paint color can be one of the most daunting parts of a painting project. I spent countless hours browsing through Behr's extensive color palette on their website. I ended up selecting a rich “Deep Ocean” blue for my living room, which was part of their Marquee line, priced at about $47 per gallon. After painting, the color looked incredibly vibrant and transformed the atmosphere of the room.

**Tip for Selection:** I utilized Behr’s online color-matching tool alongside physical swatches at Home Depot, where I bought my paint. This step proved invaluable—seeing the color in different lighting helped me avoid colors that would clash with my furniture.

### Lessons Learned from DIY Through Application

While I was excited to dive into the painting process, I did encounter some challenges. On a particularly frustrating afternoon, I noticed uneven spots after applying the first coat, primarily in the corners. It was an early lesson on the importance of using high-quality tools. After switching to a better-quality roller and edging brush—a Purdy 2-inch angle brush, priced at $12—I achieved a far more polished look.

**Mistakes to Avoid:** One mistake I made was not using painter’s tape adequately. The crisp lines were difficult to achieve without it. Investing about $7 in painter’s tape not only saved me time on touch-ups but ensured my walls had a straight edge that complemented my overall design.
