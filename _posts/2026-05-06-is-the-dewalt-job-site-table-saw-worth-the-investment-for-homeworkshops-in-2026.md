## Maintenance Tips for Longevity

### Blade Care and Maintenance

One aspect I initially overlooked was blade maintenance. After a few projects, I noticed the cuts weren’t as clean as they used to be. That’s when I learned about the importance of keeping the blade in optimal condition. 

I invested in a DeWalt 10-inch Carbide-Tipped Blade at around $50, which has become my go-to for most of my woodworking. Regularly changing and maintaining the blade made a world of difference in the quality of my cuts. I’ve started to keep a maintenance schedule, cleaning my blades after every few uses to remove resin and pitch buildup. It only takes a few minutes but really improves performance.

### Overall Care for the Table Saw

Another key maintenance tip is keeping the overall saw in good condition. I regularly check the alignment and ensure that there are no nicks or scratches on the table surface. I discovered early on that even minor imperfections could throw off precision.

Here’s my routine: I perform a quarterly deep clean by removing the blade and using a soft cloth to wipe down the table, ensuring it remains free of rust. I also lubricate any moving parts lightly with machine oil, which keeps everything running smoothly.

These simple maintenance practices have not only improved performance but also extended the life of the saw, making my initial investment feel even more justified.
