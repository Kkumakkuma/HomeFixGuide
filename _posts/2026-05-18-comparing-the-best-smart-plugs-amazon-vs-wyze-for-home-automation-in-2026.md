## How Does a Smart Plug Work?

Smart plugs function as intermediary devices that connect to your standard electrical outlets, allowing you to control them remotely via a smartphone app or smart home voice assistants. After you plug your appliance into the smart plug and connect it to your Wi-Fi, you can control it from anywhere—be it turning on a lamp while you're out or scheduling a coffee maker to brew at dawn.

**Key Features of Smart Plugs:**

- **Remote Control:** Operate devices from anywhere using a smartphone app.
- **Scheduling:** Set timers to turn devices on and off automatically.
- **Voice Control:** Integrate with assistants like Amazon Alexa or Google Assistant.

This flexibility makes smart plugs essential for home automation and can significantly enhance energy efficiency.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### Claim: Smart plugs are plug-and-play devices.

Why this matters: While setting up a smart plug is straightforward, many factors can complicate the process. I learned that the Wi-Fi compatibility restrictions can create issues, especially if your router only supports a limited range of frequencies. During my testing, a friend attempted to set up a smart plug using a dual-band router without realizing it only designated 2.4 GHz for smart devices.

### Claim: All smart plugs have the same features.

Why this matters: Wyze offers unique energy monitoring, which I found invaluable for tracking my energy expenditure. This feature doesn’t exist on the Amazon smart plug, which made me reconsider my priorities. For example, discovering that my air purifier consumed more energy than expected prompted me to adjust my usage patterns.

### Claim: Voice assistants work seamlessly with all smart plugs.

Why this matters: Although I love the convenience of voice control, I quickly discovered that Amazon smart plugs only work with Alexa, whereas Wyze connects with both Alexa and Google Assistant. This limitation alters the user's experience, especially if you're invested in another ecosystem. I had to switch to my older Google Home to control some devices after confirming this compatibility.
