## What Causes a Running Toilet?

### Poor Flapper Seal

One of the most common causes of a running toilet is a faulty flapper. When the flapper deteriorates, usually from mineral deposits or wear over time, it fails to create a tight seal over the flush valve. As a result, water continually flows from the tank to the bowl.

### Malfunctioning Fill Valve 

Another culprit is a malfunctioning fill valve. If you notice that the water in the tank doesn't stop filling, or if it fills intermittently, this is likely due to issues with the fill valve not shutting off correctly. 

### Overflow Tube Issues

An overflow tube that is too short or damaged can also contribute to a running toilet. If water from the tank is consistently flowing through the overflow tube into the bowl, it may need adjustment or replacement. 

## How Can I Fix a Running Toilet Myself?

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Turn Off the Water Supply**: Before beginning any repairs, locate the shut-off valve behind the toilet and turn it clockwise to stop the water flow.

2. **Check the Flapper**: Remove the tank cover and inspect the flapper for any signs of wear or buildup. If necessary, replace it (most cost around $10-$20).

3. **Examine the Fill Valve**: If the tank continues to fill after replacing the flapper, check the fill valve. You might have to replace it to fix the issue fully. New fill valves cost about $15-$25. 

4. **Assess the Overflow Tube**: Ensure that the overflow tube isn't blocked or damaged. Adjusting its height may solve the issue, or replace it if necessary.

5. **Test the Toilet**: Once you have made adjustments or replacements, turn the water supply back on and check if the toilet still runs.

## How Does A Flapper Work?

Flappers act as a gate in the flush mechanism. When you flush the toilet, the flapper lifts, allowing water to flow from the tank into the bowl. Once the tank level lowers, the flapper falls back and seals the flush valve. Any damage or dissolution of the flapper will prompt a leak, leading to the dreaded running toilet. 

### Incentives to Replace the Flapper

- **Cost-Effectiveness**: Flappers usually cost around $10-$20, and replacing them can save you upwards of $100 a year in water bills.
- **Simplicity**: I managed to replace my flapper in minutes without prior expertise. Following a YouTube tutorial made all the difference.
